Geoscience Through Travel

Switzerland

Personally, Switzerland was my favorite trip that I went on when I was studying abroad. GEOSCI 100 has taught me that Switzerland relates directly to geoscience because of how its landscape was shaped. For example, Switzerland has the Swiss Alps which were formed by tectonic plate collision. The African and Eurasian plates pushed together which created the mountains that are present today. I was able to see the mountains and valleys that were formed, and it really was beautiful to see these in person and now I understand how Earth’s surface has changed over time.

Morocco

Visiting Morocco was a completely different experience because of the culture shock. Morocco had landscapes and a climate that I had never experienced before like the Sahara Desert, sand dunes, and the dry climate. The Atlas Mountains are also a well known mountain range that go through the country and were also formed by tectonic activity. It was so interesting to experience both desert and mountain landscapes and see the very different environments within the same country.

Italy

Italy was the country my friends and I visited for the longest period of time because we went here for spring break. We started off in Milan which is northern Italy and ended in Naples which is southern Italy. Italy sits near the boundary of the African and Eurasian plates, which leads to tectonic activity and volcanic features. For example, I was able to see Mount Vesuvius which is considered a stratovolcano. Also, near the coastal areas I was able to see signs of erosion and deposition.
